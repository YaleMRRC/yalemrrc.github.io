Variational AutoEncoders and Topic Modeling

Oct 15, 2021 · 1 min read

Topic modeling is a powerful technique to assign appropriate distributions to words and documents. David Blei (Columbia University, JMLR 2003) proposed Drichlet distribution as prior and prposed variatinal inference to obtain hyperparameters.
However, people used Marcov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) and specifically Gibbs Sampling to approximate Bayesian Inference. While MCMC approximates exact solution it is very slow. Akash Srivastava and Charles Sutton (University of Edinburgh, ICLR 2017) introduce variational autoencoders for topic models.

Basically AutoEncoder captures non-linearity where other methods like PCA don’t. Yet, it lacks regularity and may lead to overfitting. In other words, it doesn’t capture the structure of the data. Variational AE in addition captures the structure of your data by guiding your model to generate reliable samples. You can learn variational AE using graphical processing unit (GPU) dosens of times faster over millions of documents.
